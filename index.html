Intro

My journey from physics to neuroscience and cognitive modeling

Raised in the heart of a village in Kerala, my academic journey emerged from contemplations on the meaning of self, life, and the enigma of reality. What particularly fascinated me was the intricate interplay between financial decisions, especially in a middle-class setting, and the pervasive spread of superstitious beliefs. The juxtaposition of significant life choices made with little deliberation alongside the propagation of unfounded beliefs challenged my understanding of objective truth. It compelled me to reevaluate reality through the lens of subjective beliefs, resembling the adaptive nature of a Bayesian model. This dual fascination forms the core of my research interests in neuroscience, decision-making, and cognitive modelling.

I first fell for physics during my early days at IISER Pune. In my second year, I found myself wondering if there might be something even deeper than the physical reality we probe with equations and experiments—what truly shapes the world we perceive? A conversation with a humanities professor led me to Carlos Castaneda’s Teachings of Don Juan. His vivid accounts of encounters with “allies”—plant-based guides that usher one into realms beyond the ordinary—challenged everything I thought I knew about perception and inspired me to explore how we construct our reality.

I began to see humans as extraordinary pattern–seeking engines, constantly weaving the threads of perception to fill in gaps and make sense of an ever-shifting world. That insight drew me into the study of attractor dynamics in working memory—delving into how Hopf networks can model the brain’s ability to stabilize fleeting thoughts and return to core ideas like a cognitive lodestar. From there, my curiosity branched out: I explored how decision-making unfolds within those dynamic landscapes, how our emotions color each choice, and how the social tapestry we inhabit shapes the patterns we recognize and the realities we construct.

SMdRQA

SMdRQA is a Python package designed for conducting Sliding Window Multidimensional Recurrent Quantification Analysis (SMdRQA). This specialized analysis technique allows researchers to explore temporal patterns and dependencies in multidimensional data sequences using a sliding window approach.
